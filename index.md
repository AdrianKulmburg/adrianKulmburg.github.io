### Adrian Kulmburg
## Doctoral researcher the <a href="https://www.ce.cit.tum.de/cps/members/adrian-kulmburg-msc/" class="highlighted">TUM Cyber-Physical Systems Group</a>

My research focuses on answering question that arise in discrete geometry and the area of differential equations using methods from functional analysis and complexity theory, and then apply those methods to the trajectory planning of cyber-physical systems.

_For the layperson_, my main research question can be formulated as follows: Is it possible to efficiently steer a system (such as an autonomous car, a robot, or even a chemical reaction) into a safe zone, and keep it there? For instance, if several autonomous cars are on the highway in a platooning formation, how can we control them such that they keep a safe distance, despite disturbances and measurement errors?

*For the expert:* this involves solving a containment problem, i.e., checking whether a set is contained in another. Depending on which sets are involved (polytopes, zonotopes, ellipsotopes, ...), this can be a hard problem, which oftentimes can be reformulated as the computation of certain matrix norms. Surprisingly, analyzing such problems quickly leads to the theory of tensor products of Banach spaces.
