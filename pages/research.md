**For the layperson**, my main research question can be formulated as follows: Is it possible to efficiently steer a system (such as an autonomous car, a robot, or even a chemical reaction) into a safe zone, and keep it there? For instance, if several autonomous cars are on the highway in a platooning formation, how can we control them such that they keep a safe distance, despite disturbances and measurement errors?

**For the expert**: this involves solving a containment problem, i.e., checking whether a set is contained in another. Depending on which sets are involved (polytopes, zonotopes, ellipsotopes, ...), this can be a hard problem, which oftentimes can be reformulated as the computation of certain matrix norms. Surprisingly, analyzing such problems quickly leads to the theory of tensor products of Banach spaces.
